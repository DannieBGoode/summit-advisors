More and more entrepreneurs, investors, and professionals in Europe are deciding to move their tax residence to Andorra, attracted by its competitive tax system, legal stability, and quality of life. However, transferring tax residence to another country requires proper planning. In this article, we explain the most common mistakes when changing tax residence to Andorra and how to avoid them.

**1. Thinking that spending 183 days in Andorra is enough**

One of the most common myths is that in order to have tax residence in Andorra it is sufficient to live in the country for more than 183 days during the calendar year. Although this is one of the criteria used, it is not the only one.

Tax authorities also analyze:

* where the center of economic interests is located
* where the family resides (spouse and minor children)
* where professional activity is carried out
* where companies are managed from

If most economic activity continues to take place in the country of origin, that country may still consider itself your tax residence. For this reason, it is essential to plan the relocation properly before making the move.

**2. Not properly terminating tax residence in the country of origin**

One of the most common problems when moving to Andorra for tax reasons is not properly ending the previous tax residence.

Changing residence usually involves:

* notifying the tax administration of the change
* reorganizing professional activity
* reviewing the corporate structure
* demonstrating that the center of life is in Andorra

If this process is not carried out correctly, dual tax residence may arise, which can lead to inspections or conflicts with tax authorities.

**3. Keeping the center of economic interests outside Andorra**

Many people move physically to the country but continue to have most of their economic activity elsewhere.

To determine tax residence, authorities may analyze:

* where the main income is generated
* where business decisions are made
* where the companies are located
* where the effective management of the companies takes place

If all of this occurs outside Andorra, the change of tax residence may be challenged.

**4. Not properly structuring the company before relocating**

Another common mistake when transferring tax residence to Andorra is failing to review the corporate structure beforehand.

Many entrepreneurs maintain structures designed for their previous country, which can lead to:

* inefficient tax structures
* unnecessary tax risks
* difficulties proving that management is carried out from Andorra

In many cases, it is advisable to reorganize the corporate structure before relocating.

**5. Not demonstrating real ties to Andorra**

To consolidate tax residence, it is important to demonstrate real ties with the country.

This may include:

* having a primary residence in Andorra
* spending effective time in the country
* carrying out economic activity from Andorra
* having local bank accounts and professional relationships

The clearer the connection with the country, the stronger the tax residence will be.

**6. Starting the process without international tax planning**

Relocating to another country has implications that go far beyond personal taxation.

It may also affect:

* corporate structures
* wealth planning
* dividend taxation
* international taxation

Prior planning makes it possible to optimize the transition and avoid future tax problems.

**7. Not working with specialized advisors in Andorra**

Each situation is different and depends on multiple factors:

* country of origin
* type of activity
* assets and wealth
* corporate structure

For this reason, it is advisable to work with advisors specialized in tax residence in Andorra before starting the process. This allows the relocation to be carried out correctly and helps take full advantage of the benefits offered by the Andorran tax system.

**Conclusion: Moving to Andorra requires proper planning**

Andorra has become one of the most attractive destinations in Europe for entrepreneurs and professionals seeking to optimize their tax situation and improve their quality of life. However, changing tax residence to Andorra must be done with proper planning in order to avoid problems with the tax authorities in the country of origin. Doing the process correctly from the beginning is the best way to take advantage of the opportunities the country offers.
